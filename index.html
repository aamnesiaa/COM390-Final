Description
This is a choice-driven horror game that I created using Twine (SugarCube) will leave you right in the middle of a camping tripgone wrong by a missing person trailhead, a cave system, and an increasing sense of something being off that continually draws you in multiple directions depending on what you choose to do and how it affects the outcome and whether you will get out alive.

Artist's Statement
I had this one inspiration that was just very specific: it was from the old user-generated games that I used to discover. onto online. I don’t remember all of the plot or all of “the rules” from these games, I remember what I was feeling. Rather than rejecting those blurry memories, I decided to work with them. The holes gave me the latitude to create, and that became the The creative engine of this story: it’s a storm, it’s a campsite, it’s a trailhead for a missing persons alert, and it’s the idea that something just isn slightly off no matter what you choose.

As it's an interactive experience, the player not only gets to read about poor choices, you make those choices and feel the The pressure builds when the change in weather comes into play, and the choices begin to dwindle. The presentation was made simple intentionally so that

mood transports the experience: dark hues, readable text, and large scene transitions modeled on title cards. This original project contained other images within the game environment. Unfortunately, those images were lost during build process, so the version you’re playing now is much more reliant on writing and structure. But for now, I’m still happy with that constraint because it compelled me to ensure that the presence of the atmosphere was evident through the words and the branching paths.
